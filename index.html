Exploring Australian Tourism Trends (2014-2024)

Analyzing tourist movement, reasons for visits, and state-based distribution across Australia.

Tourist Distribution by State (2014-2024)

This map shows the distribution of tourists across Australian states from 2014 to 2024, highlighting regional differences in tourist numbers. Larger circles represent states with higher tourist arrivals, indicating the most popular destinations for both domestic and international visitors.

Grouped Bar Chart: Tourist Comparison by State and Year

This chart compares the number of tourists visiting various Australian states each year from 2014 to 2024. The clear rise in tourist counts in NSW and Victoria is evident, especially post-2018, highlighting these states as the leading destinations for visitors.

This heatmap displays the reasons tourists visited Australia, categorized by their length of stay, from 2014 to 2024. Holiday travel stands out as the primary reason, while business travel and education-related visits have also remained consistent.

This heatmap shows the total number of tourists visiting each Australian state from 2014 to 2024. NSW consistently ranks as the top state for tourist arrivals, followed by Victoria and Queensland, demonstrating their strong appeal to visitors.

Pie Chart: Movement Categories by Year

This pie chart visualizes the distribution of movement categories, such as short-term and long-term visitors, from 2014 to 2024. The majority of tourist movement comes from short-term residents returning and long-term visitors, highlighting a stable pattern of frequent travel into and out of Australia.

Data Source: Australian Tourism Reports (2014-2024)
